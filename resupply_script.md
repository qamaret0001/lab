# Serenity DME — Resupply Operations: Staff Q&A Script

This document serves as a training and call-script reference for Serenity staff handling resupply operations. It covers both operational/process questions and documentation requirements.

---

## PART 1 — Operational Questions (12 Core Topics)

---

### Q1: We have no inventory. How do we purchase and deliver resupply items?

**Script:**
> "Since Serenity does not maintain physical inventory, all resupply items are fulfilled through direct drop-shipping arrangements. When a patient's order is confirmed and insurance eligibility is verified, we place the order directly with our supplier or distributor, who ships the item directly to the patient's address. This eliminates the need for warehouse storage on our end. Staff should confirm the patient's shipping address, preferred delivery window, and ensure the order is entered into the system with the correct item codes before submitting to the supplier."

---

### Q2: How will patients contact Serenity (no office) to place an order?

**Script:**
> "Because Serenity operates without a physical office, patients have multiple ways to reach us. They can call our dedicated resupply phone line, send an email, or — once available — submit an order through the Serenity website portal or mobile app. When a patient calls, greet them warmly, verify their identity using name and date of birth, confirm their current insurance information, and log the request in the system. Always provide a reference number and an estimated fulfillment timeline before ending the interaction."

---

### Q3: How does Serenity contact patients for due resupply items per insurance policy (capped within 13 months)?

**Script:**
> "Insurance policies — especially Medicare — allow resupply on a defined schedule, commonly every 90 days for supplies such as masks and filters, capped within a 13-month cycle. Serenity should maintain a proactive outreach calendar. When a patient's resupply window opens, staff should contact them via phone, text, or email with a standard resupply notification. The script should be: 'Hi [Patient Name], this is [Staff Name] from Serenity. Based on your insurance coverage, you are eligible to receive your replacement CPAP [supplies/equipment] at no additional cost. Would you like us to process that for you today?' Always document the outreach attempt in the system regardless of the outcome."

---

### Q4: For retail (out-of-pocket) orders — how will payments be received?

**Script:**
> "For patients paying out-of-pocket (retail), Serenity will accept payment through our secure online payment portal, credit/debit card over the phone, or electronic check. Staff should inform patients of the total cost upfront, including any applicable taxes or shipping fees. State clearly: 'The total for your order today is $[amount]. Would you like to pay by card, or would you prefer a link sent to your email?' Ensure all payment transactions are recorded in the billing system and a receipt is sent to the patient."

---

### Q5: Medicare/Medicaid rental closure — rental period expires in December. Will rental stop, and when?

**Script:**
> "For patients covered under Medicare or Medicaid, DME such as CPAP machines are typically rented on a 13-month rental program, after which ownership transfers to the patient and billing stops automatically. When the rental period is nearing its end in December, staff should review each account and confirm the final billing month. The rental claim should be submitted for the last eligible month, and then the account should be flagged as 'Rental Closed — Ownership Transferred.' Patients should be notified: 'Your equipment rental period has ended, and you now own your device. We can continue to supply your replacement accessories as needed.'"

---

### Q6: Will ALL private insurances remain active, or just some?

**Script:**
> "Not all private insurance contracts may remain active. Before processing any resupply order for a privately insured patient, staff must verify active contract status for that specific payer. Use the eligibility system to confirm that Serenity is still an in-network provider for that insurance plan. If coverage has lapsed or the contract is closed, inform the patient: 'It appears we are no longer contracted with [Insurance Name] at this time. We can discuss a self-pay option, or we can help you identify another provider who accepts your insurance.' Always escalate to a supervisor if unsure."

---

### Q7: Anthem HIP & BCBS MI NPI are closed — can the eligibility module be stopped via the portal?

**Script:**
> "For payers where Serenity's NPI is no longer active — specifically Anthem HIP and BCBS MI — the eligibility verification module for those payers should be deactivated to avoid unnecessary checks and potential billing errors. The billing or IT administrator should log into the respective payer portals and disable or update the NPI status accordingly. Until this is done, if a patient presents with Anthem HIP or BCBS MI insurance, staff should not process a claim and should advise: 'We are currently unable to bill your insurance plan. Please contact your insurance provider or we can assist you with a self-pay arrangement.'"

---

### Q8: For resupply — are eligibility and verification still required?

**Script:**
> "Yes — eligibility verification is mandatory for every resupply order, even for existing patients. Before shipping any item, staff must run a real-time eligibility check to confirm: (1) the patient's coverage is still active, (2) the item requested is covered under the current plan, and (3) the patient has not already received the same item within the restricted timeframe. Staff should record the eligibility confirmation number and date in the patient's account. Never skip verification, as failed verifications are a leading cause of claim denials."

---

### Q9: For the resupply process — do we still need Bonafide?

**Script:**
> "Bonafide is currently used to automate patient outreach and manage the resupply workflow. Whether to continue using Bonafide is under review. Until a final decision is made, all resupply outreach should continue using Bonafide as configured. Staff should not make changes to Bonafide workflows without written authorization from management. If Bonafide is discontinued, staff will be notified and retrained on the replacement process before any transition occurs."

---

### Q10: The most important factor — compliance records for insurance resupply

**Script:**
> "Compliance documentation is the single most critical requirement for insurance-covered resupply. Before any resupply claim is submitted, staff must confirm that the patient's file contains: (1) proof of active use of the device (e.g., CPAP compliance data showing 4+ hours/night for 70%+ of nights), (2) a valid physician order, and (3) any required CMN or LMN. Without these, the claim will be denied. Staff should pull the compliance report before contacting the patient and should not process the order if compliance thresholds are not met. Escalate to the compliance team if there is any uncertainty."

---

### Q11: Suggestion — Order placement via Serenity website or mobile app

**Script:**
> "We are in the process of enabling patient self-service through the Serenity website and mobile app. When this feature launches, patients will be able to log in, view their eligible resupply items, and place orders directly — 24/7, without needing to call. Until then, all orders must go through phone or email. When speaking with patients, let them know: 'We are working on a new online ordering option to make this even easier for you. We'll send you an update once it's available.' Collect their preferred contact method for that notification."

---

### Q12: If Bonafide is discontinued — can we develop our own software for order, sales, and claim management?

**Script:**
> "Development of an in-house platform is being evaluated as a long-term solution. Such a system would handle patient order management, inventory tracking, sales reporting, and direct insurance claim submission. This is a significant investment and will require input from clinical, billing, IT, and compliance teams. Staff should not make any commitments to patients or vendors about this system. For any patient or vendor questions, respond with: 'We are always looking to improve our systems to serve you better, and any updates will be communicated through official channels.'"

## PART 2 — Documentation Requirements for Resupply (6 New Questions)

---

### Q13: If a patient comes for resupply, what documents are required to process the order?

**Script:**
> "When a patient contacts us for resupply, the following documents must be on file and current before we can process the order:
> 1. **Valid Insurance Card / Updated Insurance Information** — confirm coverage is active.
> 2. **Physician Order (RX)** — must be on file; check if still valid or if a new one is needed.
> 3. **Compliance Report** — for CPAP/BiPAP patients, download the latest 90-day usage data to verify the patient meets insurance compliance thresholds (typically 4+ hours/night for 70% of nights over a consecutive 30-day period).
> 4. **CMN or LMN (if applicable)** — some insurances require a Certificate of Medical Necessity or Letter of Medical Necessity to be current.
> 5. **Patient Demographics & Delivery Address** — confirm shipping address and contact information are up to date.
> 
> Staff should pull the patient's chart and run through this checklist before confirming the order. If any item is missing, communicate clearly what is needed: 'Before we can ship your supplies, we need to get [specific document] on file. Here is how you can provide that…'"

---

### Q14: Do we need a new physician order (RX) for resupply, or can we use the existing prescription?

**Script:**
> "In most cases, an existing physician order can be used for ongoing resupply — provided it meets the following conditions:
> - The RX is still valid (not expired — Medicare requires orders to be renewed per their guidelines, generally every 12 months for certain items).
> - The order covers the specific item(s) being resupplied (e.g., it specifies CPAP supplies, not just the device).
> - No significant change has occurred in the patient's diagnosis, equipment, or treatment plan.
> 
> **When a NEW physician order IS required:**
> - The patient's insurance has changed.
> - The patient's diagnosis or therapy settings have changed.
> - The existing order has expired.
> - The insurance specifically requires a new order for continued resupply.
> 
> Staff should check the order date and type in the patient file before proceeding. If a new order is needed, advise the patient: 'We will need an updated prescription from your doctor before we can process this resupply. Would you like us to send a request directly to your physician's office?'"

---

### Q15: Are there any required compliance documents (CMN, LMN, or progress notes) for resupply?

**Script:**
> "Yes — depending on the payer and the type of equipment, specific compliance documents may be required:
> 
> - **CMN (Certificate of Medical Necessity):** Required by Medicare for certain DME items (e.g., PAP devices and supplies). The CMN must be completed and signed by the treating physician and must be current. For ongoing resupply, confirm whether a recertification CMN is due.
> - **LMN (Letter of Medical Necessity):** Often required by private insurances in lieu of a CMN. It should be on physician letterhead, describe the diagnosis, medical need, and the specific items requested.
> - **Progress Notes / Clinical Documentation:** Some insurances require recent office visit notes (typically within the last 6–12 months) confirming ongoing medical need.
> - **Compliance Reports (PAP Therapy):** For CPAP/BiPAP resupply, compliance data downloaded from the device (showing usage hours) is required to demonstrate the patient is actively using the equipment.
> 
> Staff should check the specific payer's documentation requirements before submitting. If any required document is missing, the order must be placed on hold: 'I see that we are still waiting on [CMN/LMN/progress notes] from your doctor. We have sent a request, and we will ship your supplies as soon as we receive it.'"

---

### Q16: What documentation is needed if the patient's insurance has changed?

**Script:**
> "If a patient's insurance has changed since the last order, the following steps and documents are required before processing resupply:
> 
> 1. **New Insurance Card** — collect the new insurance card (front and back) and update the patient's account.
> 2. **New Eligibility Verification** — run a real-time eligibility check with the new insurance to confirm coverage for the specific HCPCS codes needed.
> 3. **New Physician Order (RX)** — most insurances require a fresh prescription when a coverage change occurs.
> 4. **New CMN or LMN** — the new insurance may require its own CMN/LMN, even if one already exists on file for the previous payer.
> 5. **Prior Authorization (PA)** — the new insurance may require a prior authorization before approving the resupply claim. Submit the PA request immediately and do not ship until it is approved.
> 6. **Patient Consent & Assignment of Benefits** — obtain a new signed AOB (Assignment of Benefits) form for the new insurance.
> 
> Staff should communicate this to the patient: 'Thank you for letting us know about your insurance change. We'll need a few items from you and your doctor to get everything updated. This may take [estimated time]. We'll keep you informed every step of the way.'"

---

### Q17: What should we do if the patient does not meet compliance requirements for resupply?

**Script:**
> "If a patient does not meet the required compliance thresholds (e.g., insufficient CPAP usage data), the following steps apply:
> 
> **Step 1 — Notify the Patient:**
> Inform the patient clearly and compassionately: 'Based on the usage data from your device, your insurance requires a minimum of 4 hours of use per night for at least 70% of nights over a 30-day period in order to continue covering your supplies. Your current data shows [X hours/night on Y% of nights], which does not meet that requirement at this time.'
> 
> **Step 2 — Do NOT Ship:**
> Do not process or ship the resupply order if compliance thresholds are not met for insurance-billed orders. Shipping without documented compliance is a compliance violation and can result in claim denial and audit risk.
> 
> **Step 3 — Offer Self-Pay Option:**
> If the patient needs supplies urgently, offer a self-pay/retail option: 'If you need supplies sooner, we can process your order as a self-pay at [retail price]. Would you like to go that route while you work on building your compliance hours?'
> 
> **Step 4 — Refer to Clinical Team:**
> Flag the account and refer the patient to their physician or our clinical support team to address any therapy adherence issues. Document all actions taken in the patient's account."

---

### Q18: What steps should be followed if resupply is urgent but documentation is incomplete?

**Script:**
> "In situations where resupply is urgent (e.g., damaged equipment, medical necessity) but documentation is not yet complete, follow this escalation process:
> 
> **Step 1 — Assess Urgency:**
> Confirm the nature of the urgency. Ask: 'Can you describe the issue with your current equipment or supplies?' If the patient indicates a medical emergency, advise them to seek immediate medical care and escalate to the clinical supervisor.
> 
> **Step 2 — Identify Missing Documents:**
> Run through the documentation checklist to identify exactly what is missing. Do not ship if more than one critical document is absent.
> 
> **Step 3 — Expedite Documentation Collection:**
> - Fax or electronically send a physician order request immediately.
> - Contact the patient's doctor's office directly to expedite the CMN/LMN.
> - Inform the patient of the timeline: 'We have reached out to your doctor's office to expedite the required paperwork. Most offices respond within 24–48 hours.'
> 
> **Step 4 — Supervisor Authorization:**
> If the situation is truly urgent and a delay poses a health risk, obtain written supervisor or manager authorization to ship on a provisional basis (with documentation to follow within 48–72 hours). This must be documented in the system.
> 
> **Step 5 — Document Everything:**
> Record all steps taken, the reason for urgency, supervisor approval (if applicable), and a follow-up date to confirm all documentation is received and filed.
> 
> **Step 6 — Follow Up:**
> Ensure a follow-up call is scheduled once the documents are received to confirm the file is complete and compliant before the next resupply cycle."
